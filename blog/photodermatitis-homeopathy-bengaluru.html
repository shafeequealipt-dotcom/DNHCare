What is Photodermatitis and How Does it Affect the Skin?

Photodermatitis is a skin condition characterized by an abnormal reaction to sunlight, leading to inflammation, redness, and rashes. This condition can be acute or chronic and may be triggered or worsened by various factors, including the intense sunlight during Bengaluru's monsoon season.

The symptoms of photodermatitis can vary from person to person but often include itching, burning sensations, and the appearance of blisters or patches on the skin. If left untreated, photodermatitis can lead to long-term skin damage and increase the risk of skin infections.

Preventing Photodermatitis During Bengaluru's Monsoon Season

Prevention plays a crucial role in managing photodermatitis, especially during the monsoon season in Bengaluru. By taking a few simple precautions, individuals can significantly reduce their risk of developing this condition.

It is essential to be mindful of the time spent outdoors, especially during peak sun hours, and to use appropriate protective measures such as hats, sunglasses, and clothing that covers the skin. Additionally, applying a natural sunscreen with a suitable SPF can provide an extra layer of protection against the sun's harmful rays.
