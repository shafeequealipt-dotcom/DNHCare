Sinus Headache vs Migraine in Varthur – How to Tell the Difference

By Dr. Nafia M· 2026-06-13· 4 min read

If you live in Varthur and often wonder whether your pounding head pain is a sinus headache or a migraine, you are not alone. Understanding the key differences helps you choose the right relief and when to visit Dr. Nafia’s Homoeopathic Medical Centre.

Typical signs of a sinus headache versus a migraine

A sinus headache usually follows a cold, allergy flare‑up or a recent change in weather. The pain is often felt behind the cheekbones, forehead or bridge of the nose and worsens when you bend forward or lie down. Nasal congestion, thick yellow discharge and a feeling of pressure in the face are common companions.

Migraine, on the other hand, often starts with visual disturbances such as flashing lights or auras, and the pain is usually one‑sided, throbbing, and can last from a few hours to several days. Sensitivity to light, sound, or smells, and nausea are typical migraine accompaniments.

  • Sinus headache: pressure‑like pain, facial tenderness, worse with head‑down position.
  • Migraine: pulsating pain, unilateral, accompanied by aura, light/sound sensitivity.

How allergies can trigger each type of headache in Bengaluru

Allergic rhinitis is common in Bengaluru’s humid climate, especially during monsoon season. The swelling of nasal passages can block sinus drainage, leading to a sinus‑type headache. In some people, the same allergens (dust, pollen, mould) act as migraine triggers, provoking neuro‑vascular changes that result in a migraine attack.

  • Identify seasonal allergens – dust, pollen, mould spores.
  • Keep windows closed during high pollen days; use air purifiers at home.
  • Regular nasal irrigation can reduce sinus pressure and may lower headache frequency.

When to seek professional evaluation in Sarjapur Road and nearby areas

If headache pain is sudden, severe, or accompanied by fever, vision loss, or neurological signs, immediate medical attention is essential. Persistent headaches that do not improve with home care should be evaluated to rule out infections, dental issues, or other underlying conditions.

Common questions

How can I tell if my headache is caused by sinus congestion or a migraine?

Sinus‑related pain is usually a steady pressure behind the eyes or cheeks and gets worse when you lean forward. Migraine pain is often throbbing, on one side of the head, and may be preceded by visual aura, light sensitivity, or nausea.

Can homeopathy help with both sinus headaches and migraines?

Homeopathy provides individualized remedies that match the total symptom picture. Certain medicines are traditionally used for sinus pressure, while others suit migraine characteristics. A consultation with Dr. Nafia helps select the most appropriate remedy.

What simple steps can I take at home in Varthur to reduce headache frequency?

Keep indoor air clean with regular dusting and a HEPA filter, stay well‑hydrated, perform daily nasal saline rinses, maintain regular sleep, and limit triggers such as strong fragrances or excess caffeine.
